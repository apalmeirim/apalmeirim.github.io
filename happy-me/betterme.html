better me

about a year ago, maybe a bit more, i had suicidal thoughts. there was a genuine part of me that wanted to seize the pain from its source. i was so fed up with how i was treating myself, i wanted the most effective and simple way to solve my issue. the sadness and anger that fell upon me when looking in the mirror broke me, while i did nothing but watch it consume me. i wasn’t acting in a proactive manner and i just accepted everything for how it was, being unfair to the human being behind the actions that i partook in. depression is a serious topic, but in my case, it could’ve been easily avoided. i look back on these times and regret not spending my time wisely, and at the same time, i laugh on how miserable i was for no apparent reason.

the reasoning behind all of it was simple: i felt lost, no one helped me find a way, i couldn’t find the way by myself, i sat still and whined about how the overwhelming amount of opportunity was actually harming me and not doing me any good. it feels as embarrassing as it sounds. days, weeks, months would go by and i was growing comfortable with being unemployed, exercising by getting in and out of bed, smoking all the bad and scary thoughts away and taking little to no care for myself. motivation was in-existent, inspiration remained in dreams and confidence was at an all time high. it sounds contradictory, but this was the peak, the moments before everything falls apart. the lack of a routine, a purpose, slowly but surely reminded me that i was useless, insignificant and hopeless. i was grasping for love, happiness and direction from everywhere and everyone but inside, i just couldn’t find it. so when everything else failed, i started to take extreme measures. the fog became dense, the comfort grew and i just gave up, and kept telling myself there was no way back, i had reached a dead end. in a dramatic sense, this was a turning point. i forced myself to seek help in family and friends, and decided that i would try a psychologist, start building a routine and focusing on small but important steps.

this point in time is still a smudgy blur, a lot of little moments that made a tiny but fundamental difference. i was disciplined in taking action on habits that i saw as fun, but boring at the same time. i began exercising regularly, reading everyday, making sure that all meals were eaten and tried my best to tear away all the unnecessary worry. my psychotic lust even decided to die down, and for the first time in a long time, i was feeling satisfied with the direction my life was headed. a lot of unbearable stress was relieved and a healthy panic had settled in, reminding me that time doesn’t wait. i had a couple of motorcycle accidents, one that changed my life due to a small but lingering injury that left me grateful, not only for escaping mostly unscathed, but also for the long and painstaking recovery that taught me patience in a way i had never imagined. the rest of the story is full of fun and whimsical detail, but i started appreciating much of what i took for granted, that is the deepest conclusion. this said, my “gap” year taught me that i’m not fit for being comfortable, i get stuck and the effort to get moving was and is never worth it. negligence in accepting this fact is what punished me, it’s what made that year practically worthless, a waste of time.

not all is lost. growth is irreversible, but continuous study is needed to keep growing, otherwise we stagnate. i don’t need a rigorous plan, or some magical formula to act on this, just discipline. my job to keep myself on the right path, is choosing it whenever i fall onto the bifurcation, to not fall into temptation of the easy way out. it doesn’t require large mental effort, just making the right decision without thinking twice about how much is needed to make it. whenever i am dealt with doing something or ignoring it, i do it. between being lazy, leaving it for another day, and acting on the thought, keeping it for today, i choose to act. doesn’t get much simpler than that, and let the snowball effect take care of the rest, that’s all i need to become a better me.
