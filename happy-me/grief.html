grief

accepting how life treats you is difficult. sometimes things that seem unfair, are just meant to be. and realizing this thought-provoking reality is simple, taking it for how it is, is the tricky part. life can be beautiful, ugly and plain, all at the same time.

my heart was broken. no one broke it, it just shattered. into a million pieces. maybe even a billion. i didn’t act correctly, i didn’t act at all, and life hit me with a reality check that put me back into my place. slowly falling back into the same problem that has haunted me my whole life: the existential need of approval from external souls. i’ve realized that when i am alone, i’m weak. handling loneliness isn’t a process, it’s simply an obstacle that lives permanently on my doorstep. i am extremely needy for attention, and want to be constantly liked by everyone around me, easily molded to peoples opinions and values, without focusing on my own. i give in so easily to this weakness, i delude myself into thinking it is a strength. it’s not. and now that i truly feel alone and disconnected to the world around me, i seek comfort in things i no longer possess: a love that was never real.

it hurts to think about. all the blissful moments of joy, the uncanny lack of worry, that butterfly filling feeling of comfort. hugs, kisses, days and nights spent just living in the presence of your other half. it lasted 3 months, but time is always relative. it was amazing, life changing and a love that inspires me till this day. but i don’t have it anymore. we lost it, not just me. the pain that comes from not having closure is even deeper, like a wound left open, completely infected by hope and desire for another chance. it’s heavy, humbling and hopeless. how am i being fair to myself, running after someone that doesn’t even bat an eye to my existence anymore? how am i helping myself move on, chasing a reality that was apparently false? how am i being kind to myself, remaining inferior to someone that no longer cares about me? i’m not. it’s not a selfish act, it’s a stupid one. i’m just hurting myself, without any real reason. imagining scenarios where my lack of self empathy is justified, but it will never be. i’m wasting my time, energy and love for someone that will never acknowledge the pain i feel after losing them. i must move on, it is my only choice, the only decision, the only path i can take. that is the minimum i can do for the peace of everyone involved.

this was a lesson life has taught me: i am weak. i have little to no self value, and am most of the time in the hands of others. i need to construct a peace that cannot be broken, even by me. this grief i feel after losing “the love of my life” should impulse me into a positive direction, but it’s keeping me anchored to embarrassment. i just have to keep moving, time is always relative.
